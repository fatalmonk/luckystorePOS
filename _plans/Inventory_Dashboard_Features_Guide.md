# Inventory Management Dashboard: Essential Features Guide

## Part 1: Top 10 Core Features (All Retail Stores)

1. **Real-Time Stock Levels**
   Live visibility into current quantities across all SKUs, updated instantly with every sale, return, or receipt. No batch delays—retail moves fast.

2. **Low Stock & Reorder Alerts**
   Automated notifications when inventory drops below configurable thresholds, with suggested reorder quantities to prevent stockouts and lost sales.

3. **Sales Velocity & Turnover Analytics**
   Track how fast each product sells (units per day/week), identify slow movers, and highlight top performers to inform purchasing and promotions.

4. **Multi-Location Inventory View**
   Unified dashboard showing stock across the main store, backroom, warehouse, and any additional locations—with transfer capabilities between them.

5. **Purchase Order & Supplier Management**
   Create, track, and manage POs directly from the dashboard. Link products to suppliers, view lead times, and monitor incoming shipments.

6. **Product Categorization & Advanced Search**
   Filter by category, brand, season, price range, or custom tags. A retail store may carry thousands of SKUs—finding items quickly is critical.

7. **Barcode/RFID Scanning Integration**
   Support for scanning during receiving, stock counts, and lookups. This reduces manual entry errors and speeds up physical inventory tasks.

8. **Expiration & Batch Tracking**
   Essential for retailers selling perishables, cosmetics, or electronics. Track expiry dates, lot numbers, and FIFO/FEFO rotation to minimize waste and recalls.

9. **Demand Forecasting**
   Predict future inventory needs based on historical sales, seasonality, and trends. Helps avoid overstocking while ensuring peak-period readiness.

10. **Integrated Reporting & Exporting**
    Pre-built reports for inventory valuation, shrinkage, stock aging, and gross margin return on investment (GMROI). One-click export to PDF/CSV for accounting and vendor negotiations.

> **Bonus:** Seamless POS integration is often the backbone that makes the above features reliable, ensuring every transaction immediately reflects in inventory counts.

## Part 2: Additional Must-Have Features for Small-Medium Stores in Bangladesh

### Connectivity & Accessibility

1. **Offline-First Mode with Auto-Sync**
   Bangladesh still experiences intermittent internet and frequent power cuts. The dashboard must work fully offline (record sales, update stock, create invoices) and sync automatically when connectivity returns—without data loss or duplication.

2. **Lightweight Mobile-First Design**
   Most SMB owners in Bangladesh run shops from low-to-mid-range Android phones, not desktops. The dashboard must be a fast, responsive PWA or Android app under 10MB, optimized for small screens and 2G/3G networks.

3. **Full Bengali (Bangla) Interface & Voice Input**
   Support Bangla UI, Bangla numerals (০-৯), and Bangla voice search for product names. Many shop owners and staff are more comfortable in Bangla than English, and voice input speeds up inventory lookups during busy hours.

### Local Financial Integration

4. **Mobile Financial Service (MFS) Integration**
   Direct integration with bKash, Nagad, Rocket, and Upay for supplier payments and customer refunds. The dashboard should log MFS transaction IDs automatically against purchase orders and sales for reconciliation.

5. **Customer & Supplier Ledger (Baki/Khata Management)**
   A built-in digital ledger tracking credit sales (baki) and supplier dues is critical. In Bangladesh, SMB retail heavily relies on trust-based credit relationships. The dashboard should show running balances, due dates, and send auto-reminders via SMS/WhatsApp.

6. **Bangladesh VAT & Tax Compliance**
   Auto-calculation of VAT (Value Added Tax) at current Bangladesh NBR rates, generation of Mushak 6.3 compliant invoices, and easy tax report export for quarterly filings. This saves small shops from hiring expensive accountants.

### Communication & Usability

7. **WhatsApp & SMS Alert Integration**
   Use WhatsApp Business API or simple SMS gateways to send low-stock alerts, due payment reminders, and daily sales summaries to the owner. WhatsApp is the dominant business communication channel in Bangladesh.

8. **Photo-Based Inventory Entry**
   Allow adding products by snapping a photo and entering minimal text (name + price). This is invaluable for shops with staff who have limited digital literacy or when onboarding hundreds of unbranded local products quickly.

9. **Local Unit & Currency Formatting**
   Support Bangladesh-specific units (kg, gram, ltr, piece, dozen, foot, yard) and BDT (৳) formatting with local comma/lakh-crore numbering conventions (e.g., 1,50,000 instead of 150,000).

### Operations & Reporting

10. **Simple Employee Permission Levels**
    Most SMB shops have 1–2 cashiers and a manager. The dashboard needs dead-simple role management: Cashier (sell only), Manager (view reports + stock), Owner (full access). No complex enterprise RBAC.

11. **Daily Closing Report (DCR) in Local Format**
    A one-tap end-of-day summary showing: total sales, cash in hand, MFS collections, credit given, credit recovered, and expenses. This mirrors the traditional "daily cash memo" Bangladeshi shopkeepers maintain manually.

12. **Low-Cost, Local Payment Pricing**
    SMBs in Bangladesh are extremely price-sensitive. The dashboard should offer affordable monthly/annual BDT pricing (ideally under ৳500–1,000/month) with local payment options (bKash merchant pay), not just international credit cards.

13. **Bangla-Enabled Barcode/Label Printing**
    Generate and print price labels/barcode stickers using cheap thermal printers (common in Bangladesh markets) with Bangla product names and BDT prices, directly from the dashboard.

14. **Waste/Damage Logging (Nosto Tracking)**
    A simple feature to log expired, damaged, or stolen goods with reason codes. This helps small shops track shrinkage accurately for tax purposes and insurance claims, which is often done haphazardly on paper.

15. **Local Support & Onboarding in Bangla**
    Access to Bangla-speaking phone/chat support and simple video tutorials featuring local shop scenarios (grocery, pharmacy, electronics, clothing) dramatically improves adoption among non-tech-savvy owners.

## Bottom Line

For Bangladeshi SMB retail, the dashboard isn't just an inventory tool—it's a **digital replacement for the traditional cash memo, khata, and calculator**. The winning product feels locally built, works on patchy networks, respects local business habits, and costs less than a few cups of tea per day.
